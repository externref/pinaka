"Bhagavad Gita" which translates to "the song of God" is a part of one of the two well-known itihasas "Mahabharata". Occupying chapters 23 to 40 of the sixth book of Mahabharata, it is composed in the form of a dialogue between Arjuna, the prince who was preoccupied with morals and emotional dilemma about the despair, violence, and the number of deaths this Dharma yudha between the Pandavas and Kauravas is going to cost, and Shri Krishna, the avatar ( incarnation ) of god Vishnu who counsels him to fulfill his Kshatriya Dharma ( duty of a warrior ) to uphold Dharma through Karma.
